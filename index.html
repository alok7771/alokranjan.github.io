About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Robotics at the Research Center E. Piaggio, University of Pisa. I received my Ph.D. in Robotics from the University of Pisa in June 2025. From March to June 2024, I was a Visiting Ph.D. Researcher at Imperial College London, working on the characterisation of a mountain-goat-hoof-inspired robotic foot for quadrupedal robots.

Research interests

My work advances legged robotics along two parallel pillars: bio-inspired design and multi-modal locomotion. The bio-inspired thread translates advantageous biological foot features into robotic hardware for terrain stability; the multi-modal thread integrates diverse locomotion strategies to navigate variable environments. Together they define what to design for unstructured terrain.

That foundation now drives my postdoctoral focus on the more fundamental question of how to design — moving beyond intuition-based methods to use reinforcement learning for optimizing robot morphology, with task success as the objective. Alongside this, I mentor Ph.D. students and help prepare research papers and grants.

M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering — University of Groningen (RUG), the Netherlands

Sep 2018 – Aug 2020

Thesis ↗ — Do muscle synergies in the arm change when learning to use a multi-articulate PR-controlled prosthetic hand?

  • Calculated muscle synergies from 8-channel EMG data across multiple hand movements using Non-Negative Matrix Factorization in MATLAB.
  • Applied similarity analysis and clustering to track changes in synergy structure across 20 participants over a 15-session myoelectric prosthesis training program.
  • Findings informed the development of modular user-training exercises for more robust prosthesis control.

M.Tech. in Structures and Design — Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST), India

Aug 2014 – Jun 2016

Thesis ↗ — Analysis and Design of a Rover Suspension for Mobility on Uneven Terrain

  • Ran dynamic simulations of a rocker–bogie suspension rover, identifying overturning and bogie-flipping as critical failure modes.
  • Integrated a four-bar linkage between rocker and bogie to shift the instantaneous center of rotation, converting overturning-prone behavior into climb-prone behavior.
  • Optimized suspension geometry for smooth stair traversal up to 1.5× wheel diameter (100 mm), eliminating previous instabilities.
  • Achieved significant reductions in friction and torque requirements on slippery or uneven terrain.

B.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering — Krishna Institute of Engineering and Technology, India

Aug 2008 – Jun 2012

Thesis ↗ — Optimisation of a Disc Brake for an Automobile

  • Addressed the trade-off between rapid heat dissipation and structural integrity in disc brake design.
